# Larry — VC Demo Video Design Spec

**Date:** 2026-04-16
**Owner:** Fergus (recording) / Anna & Louis (review)
**Purpose:** Short, story-driven product demo video to send with the VC pitch deck. Watched before the deck is opened.

---

## 1. Strategic brief

### Audience
Venture capital investors evaluating Larry at the pre-seed / seed stage. They will watch this before opening the deck. Goal: earn the meeting.

### Core message
Larry is the autonomous execution layer for project management. It doesn't track work — it runs it.

### Why this approach (evidence summary)
- VC demos that convert are **60–120 seconds**. 68% of viewers finish videos under one minute.
- Investors explicitly watch short walkthroughs before opening 20-slide decks.
- The highest-converting demos (Linear, Superhuman, Config, Cursor) follow **Problem → Agitation → Solution → Proof**, use real product UI, and show **one concrete scenario** — not a feature tour.
- "Show the outcome before you show the product." Lead with transformation, not interface.
- Cut every sentence that doesn't move toward the wow moment.

### Positioning choices
- **Hero persona:** Named target company — zeb (financial services consultancy). Scenario is staged but realistic.
- **Format:** Founder-in-frame at emotional beats + screen capture + split-screen during the autonomous "break" sequence.
- **Magic moment:** The founder walks away from the desk. The work still gets done.

---

## 2. Scenario

**Sarah, PM at zeb.** Running a regulatory compliance rollout for a mid-sized European bank. She has just finished the weekly workstream sync — 55 minutes with the bank's Head of Risk, Data Lead, IT Ops, and her zeb Partner.

### What comes out of the meeting
Realistic mix — autonomous items + human-approval items:

| Outcome | Larry handles |
|---|---|
| Data team owes Risk team the updated exposure model by Thursday | Auto — new dependency linked |
| Regulator review pushed 2 weeks | **Needs approval** — deadline change |
| IT Ops flagged the API integration might slip | Auto — risk flag raised |
| Partner wants a written update to the bank's CFO | **Needs approval** — email draft |
| 6 small status updates across existing tasks | Auto — applied |

When Sarah returns from her break, the Action Centre has **3 cards** waiting — not 14.

## 4. Voiceover script (118 words)

Read conversationally, calmly. It should sound like a friend explaining, not a commercial.

> Sarah just finished her 8th meeting of the week. Somewhere in that 55 minutes, 14 things need to happen.
>
> Every other PM tool, this is where her second job starts. With Larry, it's where she leaves.
>
> Larry reads the transcript. Extracts the work. Updates what's changed. Maps the dependency between Risk and Data. Flags the IT slippage. Queues the two things Sarah actually needs to decide.
>
> When she gets back, she has a decision to make — not fourteen.
>
> A deadline shift. An email to the bank's CFO. She approves in four clicks. Larry handles the rest.
>
> The project is up to date. The team is notified. Sarah didn't write a single follow-up.

**Contingency cut line (if runtime exceeds 85s):** drop *"her 8th meeting of the week"* — softest beat.

## 5. Production checklist

### Before the shoot
- [ ] Write a realistic 2–3 page meeting transcript (bank regulator review). Pre-load Larry with it.
- [ ] Pre-populate a Larry project for "Bank X — Regulatory Compliance Rollout" with existing tasks so the Gantt looks lived-in.
- [ ] Pre-build the 3 Action Centre cards (deadline shift, email to CFO, one other). Email draft should be well-written.
- [ ] Book 2–3 teammates (Anna / Louis / Joel) for a 10-minute fake Zoom call — you only use 3 seconds of footage.
- [ ] Headphones, tidy desk, good lighting (window or one soft light).
- [ ] Phone on tripod for the water-cooler footage.

### Shoot order (suggested)
1. **Screen capture pass** — run through Larry end-to-end once, screen-record the whole flow. Get the Action Centre filling, the approvals, the Gantt updating.
2. **Desk footage** — sigh, typing close-up, walk-away, walk-back, approval close-up. Shoot each beat 3–5 times.
3. **Water-cooler footage** — 20–30 seconds of B-roll: phone-check, stretch, refill. Shot in one take.
4. **Zoom call intro** — 30s of the fake meeting. Use last 3 seconds.

### Edit
- Split screen constructed in the editor (not shot live).
- VO recorded separately, once picture lock is close.
- Quiet, minimal music bed (not loud stingers).
- End card 2 seconds. Logo + tagline + URL.

---

## 6. What to avoid

- ❌ The dashboard donut chart, the sidebar, the chatbot, email/Slack/calendar integrations, the briefing, any other project
- ❌ The words "AI", "LLM", "GPT", "machine learning"
- ❌ Feature lists, bullet points on screen, marketing jargon
- ❌ Generic royalty-free voiceover — it must be one of the founders
- ❌ Starting on a logo or UI screenshot — start on the human beat (sigh)
- ❌ Live demos — this is recorded and edited for a reason

---

## 7. Success criteria

A VC who watches this video:
1. Understands what Larry does in under 30 seconds
2. Feels the pain of the "before" state in the first 10 seconds
3. Notices that the founder is *not at the desk* when the work gets done — the positioning lands without being said
4. Can repeat one sentence of the value prop from memory after one viewing
5. Replies to the outreach email requesting a meeting

---
